Meet Me

I am a PhD student in Mayra Vidal's lab at the University of Massachusetts, Boston. I use field, laboratory, and genomic studies to better understand the diet breadth evolution in insect herbivores. I use public art and outreach to try and foster a familial realtionship among my human and non-human neighboors. I am a NSF Graduate Research Fellow and an alum of Georgetown University. Research

At UMass, we use fall webworm (Hyphantria cunea) to understand the ecological drivers of host plant use. Fall webworm exhibits vartiation in host plant use and diet breadth across its wide geographic range and between its two morphotypes, making it a good system to study how genetic variation between morphotypes and ecolgical variation among localities can influence host plant use and diet breadth. We hope to gain insight into how ecological and evolutionary processes interact to produce patterns of host use and diversification in insect herbivores.


At Georgetown, I used microsattlite markers to study the population effects of sea level rise induced habitat loss on Tumidagena minuta. We found that despite radpid and precipitous declines in habitat area and connectivity, T. minuta populations reamained large and resisted genetic differentiation and allele loss through drift.

Education and Outreach

I strive to be mentor for others in the lab and in my classes by connecting them to the resources and and oppurtunities that helped me find my way and by cultivating a lively and friendly community of scholars.


University of Massachusetts, Boston is the third most diverse four-year institution in the country. This means that our mentorhsip and outreach are especially impactful for diversity and inclusion in ecology and that we have a indespensible oppurtunity to make our research better and our community stronger by creating oppurtunites for our students. I am looking foward to doing my part to build a community around a shared interest in ecology, evolutionary biology, natural history, and enviromentalism.


UMass is located in Dorchester, a diverse working class neighboorhood in south Boston. We are excited connect our research to this community by bringing the Caterpillar Roadshow to youth spaces in Dorchester. I'm also working on some "community taxonomy" projects and to have some online content soon!
